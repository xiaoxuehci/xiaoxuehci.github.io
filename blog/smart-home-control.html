Co-design / Smart homes / Power

Who Should Hold Control?

This project rethinks empowerment and control in home automation among cohabitants through the lens of co-design.

Smart homes are often presented as individualized systems of convenience, but many domestic technologies are shared among cohabitants with different routines, responsibilities, and forms of authority. This project asks who gets to configure, override, delegate, or refuse control in home automation.

Through co-design, the work surfaces how power is distributed in everyday domestic settings and how design can create more negotiable forms of control. Rather than assuming automation is always empowering, it examines when control becomes care, burden, conflict, or exclusion.
