Participatory design / AI safety / Older adults

Hear Us, then Protect Us

This project studies deepfake scams and safeguard interventions with older adults through participatory design.

Deepfake scams are often framed as a problem of detection or individual awareness. This project starts from older adults' lived experiences instead, asking how scams are encountered, interpreted, discussed, and resisted in everyday social contexts.

Through participatory design, the work treats older adults as holders of situated knowledge rather than merely vulnerable users. It explores how safeguard interventions can better support their judgment, dignity, and agency when AI-generated deception becomes part of everyday life.
