When My Car Whispers Secrets to My Home

This project explores the opportunities and tensions of human-vehicle-home integrated ecosystems through speculative probes and everyday scenarios.

As vehicles, homes, and personal devices become increasingly connected, information can move across domestic and mobility contexts in subtle ways. This work uses probes to imagine how these connections might support convenience, coordination, and care, while also raising questions of privacy, consent, control, and contextual boundaries.

The project treats the car and the home not as separate smart environments, but as a connected ecosystem that reshapes everyday routines. Through speculative scenarios, it asks how people might negotiate what should be shared, what should remain separate, and who gets to decide.
